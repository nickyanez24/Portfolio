PROPULSION DESIGN

Space Launch Vehicle Design

Conceptual launch vehicle development with a focus on propulsion system sizing and architecture.

This project was completed as part of Space Launch Vehicle Design and Analysis. The objective was to develop a launch vehicle capable of delivering 250,000 pounds of payload to low Earth orbit while satisfying mission, schedule, and budget requirements through a formal Preliminary Design Review (PDR) and Critical Design Review (CDR) process.

What I Worked On

  • Served as the propulsion engineer for the launch vehicle design team.
  • Selected propulsion architecture, propellants, and engine cycle.
  • Performed engine sizing and thrust calculations.
  • Determined engine count and stage-level propulsion requirements.
  • Conducted vehicle sizing studies using first-order launch vehicle approximations.
  • Supported vehicle performance trades and design reviews.

Technical Highlights

  • Selected a LOX/Methane propulsion system using an oxygen-rich staged combustion cycle.
  • Sized propulsion systems to achieve a target launch thrust-to-weight ratio of 1.5.
  • Developed stage-level thrust requirements based on vehicle gross liftoff weight.
  • Contributed to propulsion architecture decisions including cooling methods, injector design, and nozzle sizing.

Tools Used

MATLAB, launch vehicle design, propulsion analysis, systems engineering, engineering trade studies
