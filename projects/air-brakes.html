Air Brakes System

Active altitude control using deployable aerodynamic braking surfaces.

Overview

As part of Longhorn Rocketry's Advanced Research team, I contributed to the development of a deployable air brake system designed to actively control rocket altitude. The goal of the project was to reduce apogee and improve altitude accuracy by increasing aerodynamic drag during ascent.

What I Worked On

  • Participated in the design and development of a servo-actuated air brake system.
  • Evaluated multiple deployment geometries and flap configurations.
  • Performed aerodynamic analysis to estimate drag increases at various deployment levels.
  • Conducted CFD studies to compare drag performance.
  • Performed structural analysis and design validation through FEA.
  • Supported prototype development and manufacturing planning.

Technical Highlights

  • Designed for active altitude targeting on a competition rocket with a target apogee of 10,000 feet.
  • Analyzed drag characteristics at multiple deployment positions.
  • Used CFD to evaluate aerodynamic performance before manufacturing.
  • Applied FEA to verify structural integrity under expected flight loads.

Tools Used

CFD, FEA, OpenRocket, CAD, aerodynamic analysis, structural analysis, rocket systems design
