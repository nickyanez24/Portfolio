EXPERIMENTAL TESTING

Imapct Measurement

Sensor integration, circuitry design, and experimental impact testing.

Overview

This project was completed as part of Electromechanical Systems and focused on evaluating how different packaging materials influence impact loading during drop events. Using an instrumented payload, our team collected and analyzed experimental data to compare packaging performance.

What I Worked On

  • Designed and assembled circuitry for sensor integration.
  • Integrated a piezoelectric accelerometer and photodiode with a DAQ system.
  • Developed the experimental drop-test setup.
  • Configured LabVIEW data acquisition workflows.
  • Processed and analyzed acceleration data from impact events.
  • Compared packaging materials including shredded paper, packing peanuts, and bubble wrap.

Technical Highlights

  • Collected transient impact measurements at high sampling rates.
  • Converted raw sensor voltages into acceleration data using calibration relationships.
  • Used experimental testing to compare cushioning effectiveness.
  • Demonstrated that shredded paper provided the greatest reduction in peak acceleration.

Tools Used

LabVIEW, DAQ systems, circuitry design, breadboarding, piezoelectric accelerometers, photodiodes, signal processing, experimental testing
