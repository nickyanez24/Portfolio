ASTRODYNAMICS

Spacecraft Dynamics

Orbital targeting, ground track generation, and perturbation modeling.

This project focused on implementing the Skopinski-Johnson targeting algorithm to generate an orbit capable of passing over a specified ground target after a given number of revolutions.

The project combined orbital mechanics, coordinate transformations, numerical propagation, Earth rotation, and J2 perturbation modeling to validate the resulting ground track.

What I Worked On

  • Implemented the Skopinski-Johnson targeting algorithm in MATLAB.
  • Generated orbital elements from launch conditions, target location, and mission constraints.
  • Converted orbital elements into Earth-Centered Inertial position and velocity state vectors.
  • Propagated the orbit over multiple revolutions while accounting for J2 perturbations.
  • Converted propagated states into latitude and longitude ground tracks.
  • Validated the final orbit by comparing the ground track against the desired target location.

Technical Highlights

  • Connected analytical orbital targeting methods with numerical orbit propagation.
  • Included Earth rotation effects to map inertial spacecraft motion to Earth-fixed ground tracks.
  • Used J2 perturbation modeling to improve targeting fidelity over multiple revolutions.
  • Verified mission success through ground track visualization and target overflight analysis.

Tools Used

MATLAB, orbital mechanics, numerical simulation, coordinate transformations, J2 perturbation modeling, ground track analysis
