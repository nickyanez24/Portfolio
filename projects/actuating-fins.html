Aerial Robotics

Autonomous quadrotor simulation, perception, planning, and control.

Overview

As part of Longhorn Rocketry's Advanced Research team, I led the early development of an active fin control system designed to provide roll control during flight. The long-term goal of the project is to develop technologies that could eventually evolve into fully actuated fins capable of both roll and pitch control on future competition rockets.

What I Worked On

  • Defined system requirements and overall project direction.
  • Developed OpenRocket vehicle models for aerodynamic analysis.
  • Performed sizing studies for fin tabs and control surfaces.
  • Researched and downselected candidate servo actuators.
  • Calculated expected aerodynamic moments and required actuator torque.
  • Created CAD models of fin concepts and actuator integration schemes.
  • Manufactured prototype fin designs using additive manufacturing.

Technical Highlights

  • Estimated aerodynamic loading using maximum flight velocities predicted in OpenRocket.
  • Performed torque calculations to determine minimum actuator requirements.
  • Balanced control authority against manufacturing constraints and fin thickness limitations.
  • Developed prototype hardware for future testing and controls integration.

Tools Used

OpenRocket, SolidWorks, CAD, 3D Printing, MATLAB, actuator sizing, aerospace controls, engineering analysis
